Definitions of Markov Chains
Definition 1
A Markov chain is an integer-time process, {Xn, n ⩾ 0}, for which the
sample values for each r.v. Xn, n ⩾ 1, lie in a countable set S and depend
on the past only through the most recent r.v. Xn−1.
Pr{Xn|Xn−1, . . . , X0} = Pr{Xn|Xn−1}.

Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors of Stochastic Matrices
Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors of Stochastic Matrices
A matrix [A] is singular ⇔ there is a vector v ̸= 0 such that [A]v = 0 ;
⇔ the columns of [A] are linearly dependent;
⇔ the rows of [A] are linearly dependent;
⇔ the determinant det[A] of [A] is 0.
Thus λ is an eigenvalue of [P] if and only if [P − λI] is singular.
Definition 3
A row vector π is a left eigenvector of [P] of eigenvalue λ if π ̸= 0 and
π[P] = λπ, i.e.,∑
i πiPij = λπj for all j.
A column vector ν is a right eigenvector of [P] of eigenvalue λ if ν ̸= 0
and [P]ν = λν, i.e.,∑
j Pijνj = λνj for all i.
For every stochastic matrix [P], [P]e = e and thus [P − I] is singular and
there is an eigenvalue λ = 1 .
